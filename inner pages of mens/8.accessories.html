how to choose accessories

Step-01 : Consider the Occasion

Think about the occasion and the outfit you'll be wearing. The accessories should complement the overall look and feel appropriate for the event.

Step-02 : Choose a Watch:

Start with a watch, as it's both functional and stylish. Consider the style, color, and material that would best complement your outfit. A classic watch with a leather or metal strap is versatile and suitable for various occasions.

Step-03 : Select a Belt

Choose a belt that matches the color and style of your shoes. If you're wearing black shoes, opt for a black belt, and the same goes for brown shoes. The belt should also complement the overall aesthetic of your outfit.

Step-04 :Consider a Pocket Square

For formal events or when wearing a blazer or suit jacket, a pocket square can add a pop of color and personality to your outfit. Choose a pocket square that complements the color palette of your attire without being too matchy-matchy.

Step-05 :Accessorize with Jewelry

If you like wearing jewelry, choose pieces that suit your personal style and the occasion. This could include rings, bracelets, or necklaces. Keep it understated and avoid wearing too many pieces at once to prevent your look from becoming overwhelming.

optional accessories steps for men:

  • a Hat:

    Depending on the occasion and your personal style, you may choose to wear a hat. A classic fedora or a baseball cap can add a stylish touch to your outfit. Just ensure that the hat complements the rest of your attire and fits well with the overall aesthetic.

  • Add a Tie or Bow Tie:

    If the occasion calls for it, consider adding a tie or bow tie to your outfit. Choose one that complements the color and pattern of your shirt and jacket. A tie can add a touch of sophistication to formal attire.

  • Consider a Scarf:

    In colder weather, a scarf can be both practical and stylish. Choose a scarf in a color or pattern that complements your coat or jacket. A wool or cashmere scarf can add warmth and texture to your ensemble.
