matte make-up

Step-01 : Cleanse and Moisturize

Start with a clean face. Use a gentle cleanser to remove any dirt, oil, or makeup residue. Follow up with an oil-free moisturizer to hydrate your skin without adding shine.

Step-02 : Prime

Apply a mattifying primer all over your face. This helps to control oil production and creates a smooth base for your makeup, ensuring it lasts longer.

Step-03 : Foundation

Choose a matte foundation that matches your skin tone. Apply it evenly over your face using a makeup sponge, brush, or your fingers. Blend well, paying extra attention to areas where you tend to get oily.

Step-04 : Concealer

Use a matte concealer to cover any blemishes, dark circles, or imperfections. Apply it sparingly and blend it seamlessly into your foundation using a small brush or makeup sponge.

Step-05 : Powder

Set your foundation and concealer with a translucent or matte setting powder. Use a large, fluffy brush to lightly dust the powder over your entire face, focusing on areas prone to shine, such as the T-zone.

Step-06 : Contour and Bronzer

If desired, use a matte contour powder or bronzer to add dimension to your face. Apply it to the hollows of your cheeks, along your jawline, and around your hairline to create shadows and warmth. Blend well to avoid harsh lines.

Step-07 : Blush

If you want to add a pop of color to your cheeks, choose a matte blush. Apply it to the apples of your cheeks and blend it out towards your temples for a natural flush of color.

Step-08 : Eyeshadow

For your eyeshadow, opt for matte shades in neutral tones or colors that complement your look. Apply a light shade over your entire lid as a base, then add depth with darker shades in the crease and outer corner. Blend well for a seamless finish

Step-09 : Eyeliner and Mascara

Line your eyes with a matte eyeliner for definition, if desired. Then, finish off your eye makeup with a few coats of mascara on your upper and lower lashes.

Step-10 : Eyebrows

Fill in your eyebrows using a matte eyebrow powder or pencil. Shape and define your brows to frame your eyes and complete your look.

Step-11 : Lips

Choose a matte lipstick or lip stain in a shade that complements your makeup look. Apply it evenly to your lips, starting from the center and blending outwards for a precise finish.

Step-12 : Setting Spray

Finish off your matte makeup look with a few spritzes of a matte setting spray. This will help lock in your makeup and control oil throughout the day.
