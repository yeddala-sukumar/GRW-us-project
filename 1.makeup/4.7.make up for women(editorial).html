editorial make-up

Step-01 : Prep the Skin

Start with a clean, moisturized face. Apply a primer suitable for your skin type to create a smooth base for makeup application and to help makeup last longer.

Step-02 : Foundation

Choose a foundation that matches your skin tone. Apply it evenly over your face and blend well using a makeup sponge, brush, or your fingers. Make sure to blend down your neck for a seamless finish.

Step-03 : Concealer

Use a concealer that matches your skin tone to cover any blemishes or imperfections. Additionally, use a lighter concealer under your eyes to brighten and conceal dark circles. Blend the concealer seamlessly into your foundation.

Step-04 : Contour and Highlight

Sculpt your face using contour and highlight products. Apply a contour shade to the hollows of your cheeks, along your jawline, and around your hairline to create shadows and definition. Then, use a highlighter on the high points of your face, such as the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow, to add dimension and luminosity.

Step-05 : Eyebrows

Fill in your eyebrows using a brow pencil, powder, or gel to define and shape them. For editorial looks, you may opt for bold, exaggerated brows or unique shapes to complement the overall theme of your makeup.

Step-06 : Eyeshadow

Get creative with your eyeshadow by using bold colors, textures, and techniques. Experiment with different shapes, graphic lines, and abstract designs to make a statement. You can use cream eyeshadows, pigments, glitter, or even unconventional materials like rhinestones or sequins to add interest to your eye makeup.

Step-07 : Eyeliner

Define your eyes with eyeliner to enhance their shape and add drama. You can create graphic lines, geometric shapes, or intricate designs using liquid or gel eyeliner. Feel free to experiment with different winged eyeliner styles or negative space techniques to make your eyes pop.

Step-08 : Mascara

Apply several coats of mascara to your upper and lower lashes to add volume, length, and definition. You can also use false eyelashes for added drama and impact.

Step-09 : Lips

Apply several coats of mascara to your upper and lower lashes to add volume, length, and definition. You can also use false eyelashes for added drama and impact.

Step-10 : Finishing Touches

Set your makeup with a setting spray to ensure it stays in place throughout the day or photo shoot. You can also use a highlighter to add a final touch of glow to your cheekbones, brow bone, and other high points of your face.
